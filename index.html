    COURSE CONTENT



    Definition of library, history of libraries in ancient, medieval and modern times, factors determining the establishment and patterns of library services in society with particular references to developing countries, the library as a medium of communication and its relationships with other information and communications system, functions of different types of libraries. Early writing materials frompapyrus to codex, the impact of renaissance and the invention of printing, book composition, causes of deterioration to paper and book process



    COURSE DESCRIPTION

    This is a course that will enlighten the students on the history of libraries from the ancient to medieval and to modern times. Students will be exposed to the factors determining the establishment and patterns of library services in society with particular references to developing countries, the library as a medium of communication and its relationships with other information and communicatio ns systems. Students will be introduced to the functions of different types of libraries. Attention will also be focused on the early writing materials from papyrus to codex, the impact of renaissance and the invention of printing, book composition, causes of deterioration to paper and book process.

    COURSE JUSTIFICATION

    This course is designed to educate and expose students to the history of libraries from the ancint to medieval and to modern times, factors determining the establishment and patterns of library services in society with particular references to developing countries, the library as a medium of communication and its relationships with other information and communication systems. Students will also be equipped with the knowledge of the functions of different types of libraries, early writing materials from papyrus to codex, the impact of renaissance and the invention of printing, book composition, causes of deterioration to paper and book process.



    WRITING


    Writing is the representation of sound and speech in meaningful form and in graphic form on papyrus, stone, vellum or parchment, or on similar objects. Through the alphabet, we make a combination of letters to spell out every sound that we make when speaking

FORMS OF WRITING 1. Picture writing/Hieroglyph: Picture writing is simply the use of symbols to denote words. Humankind communicated his ideas through drawing and painting them. Ancient Egyptians practiced picture writing over six thousand years ago. Their writing was called hieroglyph or holy engraving or sacred writing. 2. Cuneiform Writing: cuneiform is written by impressing wedge-shaped pen or stylus into tablets of soft clay. This method of writing was in use by the Sumerians, Babylonians, Assyrians and Persians from around 3000 B.C. until sometime after 500 B.C. when it was abandoned in favour of alphabet writing on papyrus. 3. The Alphabet: the alphabet writing in current use came to us through Latin and Greek script through the Phoenician script. A 32 letter alphabet based upon a simplified cuneiform, was found in Ugarit in the middle of the 2nd millennium B.C. Phoenicia is the Greek name for land of Canaanites (modern Lebanon). The Phoenicians invented alphabetic writing in the second millennium B.C. the Greek borrowed these Canaanites scripts and adapted certain of the signs into vowel signs at about 800 B.C. It subsequently spread all over the world.
